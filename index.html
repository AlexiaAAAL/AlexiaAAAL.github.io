NMDA Receptor Knowledge

Structure Function Pharmacology References

Structure

NMDA receptors are tetrameric ion channels composed of GluN1 and GluN2 (A-D) subunits. They have large extracellular ligand-binding domains and transmembrane domains forming the ion pore.

Function

NMDA receptors mediate calcium influx and are critical for synaptic plasticity, learning, and memory. Their activation requires binding of glutamate and co-agonists like glycine, along with membrane depolarization.

Pharmacology

NMDA receptor activity can be modulated by endogenous ligands and exogenous drugs such as ketamine, memantine, and dextromethorphan, which have clinical relevance in anesthesia, neuroprotection, and psychiatry.
